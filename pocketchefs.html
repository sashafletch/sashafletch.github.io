A Redesign

Pocket Chefs provides affordable culinary professionals to assist families with their everyday cooking needs. The process of redesiging the web and mobile platforms was meant to better engage users through the booking process and increase the conversion rate of new registrants.

    • The Challenge

    Pocket Chefs had gone through several design iterations when I was contracted as the UX Designer. The challenge for me was to maintain the aesthetic look of the initial site but to modernize and tighten the user flow. This meant restructuring the site for easier navigation without much time for in-depth user testing. Furthermore, I had to align my design with my client's preferences and the capabilities of Wix, the platform that would be used for shipping the final product.

    The Solution

    Pocket Chefs new design condensed unnecessary webpages for easier user navigation. The design focused on creating clearer calls-to-action and providing concrete information to establish trust and social proof. In order to conform to Wix's capabilities, the overall design was kept simple but was modernized for better user engagement.

    User Research

    Some user research had been performed prior to my contracted position to identify key pain points.

    Key Pain Points

    • Product is unclear: cooking lessons or personalized chef?
    • Users unclear how to navigate through website
    • No visible pricing information
    • User unsure about the differences in plans
    • Uncertainty around payment process
    • Customer unsure what resources need to be provided for chef

    User Personas

    While full user personas were not developed for the sake of time, the target audience was clearly identified to ensure that the design was meeting their overall needs and aims.
