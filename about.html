Who is Sasha?

I'm originally from Florida, but I now live in beautiful Oakland, CA! Read on to learn more about my journey to UX Design.

My Introduction to Design

My time as a teacher introduced me to the iterative design process; I created a systematic approach to mapping content to learning objectives. Each objective was defined by assessment strategies and content analysis that then informed my design process moving forward. It consistently demanded that I think creatively to solve problems of misunderstanding, behavior intervention, and classroom management.

Curriculum design is an iterative and dynamic process in which teachers must account for students’ different learning styles, levels of understanding, and paces of growth. As such, I consistently put myself into the shoes of my students and re-evaluated how my methods either aided their journey or exacerbated misunderstandings. I carry this same process into how I approach the user experience.

The Importance of Design

I have always sought fields that focus on improving other’s lived experiences through communication and analysis. And, along with that, came a strong interest for me in understanding the influences behind individual behavior and thinking.

I carry this fascination and intent into my process as a UX designer. During my mentorship program, I developed my abilities to solve real user problems by conducting thorough user research, incorporating familiar UI patterns, and consistently reiterating my designs based on user testing, feedback, and constructive criticism.
